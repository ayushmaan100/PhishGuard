Real-time phishing
protection, explained

PhishGuard analyzes every site you visit across multiple layers — before you interact with it. Here's exactly how it works.

Detection layers
0
Trusted site check Instant
The top 10,000 most-visited sites are pre-loaded. Google, Amazon, YouTube — they skip all analysis and get an instant green badge.
1
URL pattern analysis <5ms
13 signals extracted from the URL itself — typosquatting (paypa1.com), suspicious TLDs, IP addresses, @ tricks, random-looking domain names. Shows a preliminary badge immediately.
2
Google Safe Browsing ~100ms
Checks against Google's database of known phishing and malware sites. A hit here immediately shows a DANGEROUS warning, regardless of other signals.
3
Domain intelligence Parallel
Looks up domain registration age (RDAP), SSL certificate age (Certificate Transparency), and site ranking (Tranco top lists). Phishing domains are always new — this catches zero-day attacks.
4
Page content analysis Parallel
Analyzes the actual page: does the title claim to be PayPal? Does a login form submit to a different domain? Does the favicon load from the real brand's server? 8 DOM-level signals.
What the badges mean
Safe
No threats detected. Browse normally.
Suspicious
Some unusual signals. Review with caution.
High Risk
Multiple phishing indicators. Do not enter credentials.
Dangerous
Confirmed or high-confidence phishing. Leave immediately.
Privacy & data
URLs you visit are sent to Google Safe Browsing for blacklist checking. Google's privacy policy applies. This is disclosed here and in our privacy policy.
Domain age lookups send only the domain name (e.g., "evil.com"), never the full URL, to RDAP and certificate transparency servers.
Page content analysis (forms, title, favicon) happens entirely inside your browser. This data never leaves your device.
We never sell data. We never track which sites you visit for advertising. Results are cached locally in your browser only.
